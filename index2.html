Queering the

This is a space of annotating bibliographies where we respond to texts in whatever form feels most intuitive after an immediate free association. Feel free to engage with others' arts.

Taking seriously Sedgwick's notion of reparative reading alongside Huffer's imagery of the one-handed reading of the masturbating dyke, this project frames queer academic reading as iterative, excessive, and affectively charged rather than interpretively exhaustive. Drawing on Snaza, it situates the erotic as a site of struggle over joy and the conditions of emergence, emphasizing that the affective field of the reading remains irreducible to full intelligibility. Following Quijano, knowledge is understood not as a relation between subject and object, but as an intersubjective relation oriented toward shared purposes. Against the disciplinary tradition of annotating literature through logical reasoning and academic precision, the project proposes immediate free association as an interpretive method. Readers are invited to respond through forms that register affective emergence — be it poetry, sketches, music-making, or others — thereby allowing meaning to be generated beyond the constraints of productivity and propriety. Inspired by surrealist commitments to the emancipation of desire and the unconscious as new poetic weapons for social transformation, this practice treats reading queer academic literature as a transformative rather than an extractive process. In response to Huffer's call to further explore the links among autoeroticism, knowledge, and relationality as they resonate with queer feminist theory, it envisions a heterotopian reading/writing collective space in which readers encounter the desubjectivating processes of others as ethically inaccessible yet capable of reshaping one's own reading conditions. It also seeks to bring theory home as a reparative resource by stripping it of its serious connotations and reframing reflection as a more meditative practice.
